Sunlight Exposure Guide

Understanding how light travels through your home is the key to a thriving indoor garden. Align your collection with the natural rhythm of the sun.

Pro Tip

The Orientation Secret

North-facing windows provide consistent but weak light, perfect for low-light lovers. South-facing windows offer the most intense, direct rays.

North Window

Cool, indirect glow

South Window

Hot, direct energy

Morning Radiance

Best for East-facing placements

Sunlight Meter High

Place directly on a South-facing windowsill. Loves the heat and high intensity rays.

Fiddle Leaf Fig

Sunlight Meter High

Needs lots of light to maintain those large leaves. South or West windows are best.

Monstera

Sunlight Meter Medium

3-5 feet away from a South window or right by an East window. No direct scorch.

Marble Queen Pothos

Sunlight Meter Medium

Adaptable, but needs brightness to keep its white variegation. Great for shelves.

ZZ Plant

The champion of dim rooms. Can survive far away from any window source.

Still Unsure?

Light changes with the seasons. A South window in winter might be perfect, but too hot in summer. Use our daily light tracker to record your plant's energy absorption over 24 hours.
